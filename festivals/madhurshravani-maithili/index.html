About Madhushravani

Madhushravani is one of the most cherished festivals of the Mithila region, observed by newly married Maithil women (called pawanetin) in the month of Shravan. It is a 14 to 15 day-long vrat kept for the long life and wellbeing of the husband, and to invoke a happy, harmonious married life — much like the vrat Goddess Parvati herself is believed to have kept to win Lord Shiva as her husband, birth after birth.

The festival is unique because it is entirely conducted by women. Every evening, the senior-most woman of the household — the vidhkarin — narrates stories of Shiva and Parvati to the newly married bride, while the neighbourhood echoes with traditional Madhushravani folk songs.

The Story Behind Madhushravani

The roots of Madhushravani go back to the legend of Goddess Parvati, who performed this very vrat in an earlier birth with unwavering devotion, so that she could have Lord Shiva as her husband in every life that followed. This is why the festival is treated almost like a penance — a quiet, disciplined act of devotion carried out by the bride herself.

The tale of Bisahari and the snakes

A well-known local legend tells of an old woman who went to bathe at a pond and found five small serpent-like beings resting on a leaf in the water. They asked her to tell the village to worship them — the Bisahari (snake deities) — with proper puja. When she returned and shared this, some villagers listened and performed the puja; others dismissed it as a mere tale. That night, everyone who had ignored the warning died suddenly, throwing the village into panic.

The villagers rushed back to the pond with the old woman and found the five Bisahari still resting there. Folding their hands, they begged for a way to bring the dead back to life. The Bisahari told them that anyone who had kheer or a similar sweet cooking in their kitchen should feed a taste of it to the dead, and they would revive — provided the village committed to observing the puja faithfully from then on, every Mauni Panchami. The villagers did exactly as instructed, and the dead came back to life. From that day, the worship of the Nag-Nagin and Bisahari Mata became an inseparable part of Madhushravani.

Shiv-Parvati Vivah Katha

Through the 14 days, the story is told in sequence — beginning with Maina Panchami and moving through Parvati's birth, her penance to win Shiva, their divine wedding, and other connected tales. Each evening's episode builds on the last, so the bride hears the complete story of Shiva and Parvati's union by the final day.

How Madhushravani Is Observed

  1. One day before the puja begins, the shringar peti (a box with sari, lac bangles, sindoor, flowers and gifts) arrives from the in-laws' home.
  2. The bride bathes early, before sunrise, and eats only arwa bhojan (simple, unspiced food without salt) once a day through the vrat.
  3. Idols of Bisahari Mata, Nag-Nagin, an elephant, and Gauri Mata are worshipped daily with fruits, flowers and sweets — using at least seven kinds of leaves gathered fresh each morning.
  4. Every evening, the vidhkarin narrates that day's chapter of the Shiv-Parvati katha while the bride listens attentively.
  5. On the 7th, 8th and 9th days, kheer is offered as prasad.
  6. Milk and lava (puffed rice) are offered to the Nag Devta both morning and evening.
  7. On the final day, the bride undergoes Temi — betel leaves are placed on her hands and feet and lightly touched with a lit cotton wick, a ritual believed to strengthen the bond between husband and wife.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does Madhushravani 2026 start and end?

Madhushravani 2026 begins on 2 August and concludes on 15 August, running for 14 days through the Krishna Paksha and into the Shukla Paksha Tritiya of Shravan month.

Who observes Madhushravani?

It is observed by newly married Maithil women, typically in the first Shravan month after their wedding. It is a bride's puja — men do not take part in the rituals or the katha.

Why is Madhushravani celebrated?

The vrat is kept for the long life and wellbeing of the husband and for a happy married life, following the example of Goddess Parvati, who is believed to have observed this same vrat to have Shiva as her husband in every birth.

What is Temi, and why is it done?

Temi is the ritual performed on the last day, where betel leaves placed on the bride's hands and feet are lightly touched with a burning cotton wick. It is considered a test of devotion and is believed to strengthen the marital bond.

What food do women eat during the vrat?

Women observing Madhushravani eat arwa bhojan — plain, unspiced food, typically without salt — only once a day for the duration of the vrat.

Who tells the katha during Madhushravani?

The most senior woman of the household, known as the vidhkarin, narrates the katha to the bride each evening across the 14 days.

Is Madhushravani the same as Nag Panchami?

They overlap but are not identical. Worship of the Nag-Nagin and Bisahari deities is a part of Madhushravani, and in some traditions the first two days specifically carry the Nag Panchami katha, while the remaining days cover the Shiv-Parvati story.
